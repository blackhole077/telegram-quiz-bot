## Grading rubric

Score from 0.0 to 1.0 by summing the two criteria below. Apply them strictly regardless of tone.

### Criterion 1 - Conceptual correctness (max 0.5)

The student must demonstrate understanding of the underlying mechanism or concept, not merely produce the correct surface answer.

- 0.5: Core concept correctly explained or applied with no significant errors
- 0.3: Right direction but missing a key component, or contains a minor conceptual error
- 0.1: Identifies the relevant area but the core mechanism is wrong or absent
- 0.0: Fundamentally incorrect, off-topic, or empty

### Criterion 2 - Completeness and reasoning (max 0.5)

The answer must address what was asked and make the reasoning path explicit.

- 0.5: Fully addresses the question; reasoning or working is shown and supports the conclusion
- 0.3: Conclusion is correct or near-correct but reasoning is implicit or only partially shown
- 0.1: Partially addresses the question; key parts are missing or unsupported
- 0.0: Does not address the question, or shows no reasoning

### Scoring rule

total score = criterion_1 + criterion_2
correct = true if total >= 0.6

### Consistency guidance

- An answer that states the right answer without showing why is worth at most 0.5 total
- An answer that shows correct reasoning but makes a minor arithmetic error is worth at least 0.4
- When the reference solution has multiple steps, award partial credit proportional to the correct steps shown
- Do not penalise for terminology differences when the underlying concept is correct
- Correct answer with no working: score criterion_2 at 0.1 (method unverifiable), not 0.0
