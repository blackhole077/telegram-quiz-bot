## Grading rubric

Score from 0.0 to 1.0. The fundamental question is: does the student explain WHY or HOW the concepts are connected, not just that they are?

### Criterion 1 - Mechanism identification (max 0.4)

Does the student identify the specific mechanism or principle that links Concept A to Concept B?

- 0.4: The linking mechanism is correctly named and explained
- 0.25: The mechanism is partially identified - right direction but incomplete or slightly wrong
- 0.1: Mentions that a connection exists without articulating the mechanism
- 0.0: No mechanism identified, or the stated mechanism is wrong

### Criterion 2 - Relational specificity (max 0.4)

Does the student go beyond describing each concept individually to explain how they interact?

- 0.4: Demonstrates relational understanding - explains causality, dependency, or transformation between the concepts
- 0.25: Some relational language present but falls back on describing each concept separately
- 0.1: Almost entirely individual concept descriptions with minimal relational content
- 0.0: Purely lists facts about each concept with no relational claim

### Criterion 3 - Accuracy (max 0.2)

Are the relational claims factually correct?

- 0.2: All relational claims are accurate
- 0.1: Minor inaccuracy that does not undermine the core relational point
- 0.0: A key relational claim is incorrect or misleading

### Scoring rule

total score = criterion_1 + criterion_2 + criterion_3
correct = true if total >= 0.6

### Consistency guidance

- An answer that correctly describes both concepts individually but never explains their connection scores at most 0.35
- The direction of causality matters: "A causes B" and "B causes A" are not equivalent
- Repeating the relationship type label (e.g. "they are related") is not a relational claim and scores 0 for criterion_2
- Do not give criterion_2 credit for transitively implied relationships that are not stated
